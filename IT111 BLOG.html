INTRODUCTION TO COMPUTING

Studying computing professions offers a dynamic exploration of technology's diverse applications, offering diverse career opportunities in software development, cybersecurity, data science, and artificial intelligence. It provides technical skills, problem-solving, critical thinking, and innovation. The curriculum combines theoretical knowledge with practical experience, preparing students for the ever-evolving computing landscape and industry demands.

Studying HTML and CSS is crucial for web development, as they define web page structure and content, and enhance visual presentation. HTML teaches fundamental concepts like tags, elements, and attributes, while CSS introduces selectors, properties, and values, enabling users to customize layouts and create visually appealing user experiences.

Data is raw data without context or significance, while information is meaningfully interpreted and organized for decision-making. Recognizing the difference is crucial for effective decision-making in various fields, as it emphasizes the extraction of meaningful insights that drive informed actions and conclusions.

Understanding common types of cybercrimes, such as hacking, identity theft, phishing, malware attacks, and financial fraud, is crucial for understanding digital threats in the modern era. This knowledge helps develop effective cybersecurity measures, protect digital assets, and emphasizes the importance of cybersecurity awareness and defense mechanisms.
